Fact Attack: Entertaining Nuggets of Knowledge

The Smelly "Socks" Airline: In 1971, Icelandair started giving out scratch-and-sniff postcards to passengers, scented like sulphur, to prepare them for the country's distinct geothermal aroma.

Flight Phobias: Around 40% of people on flights admit they don’t know how to use the airplane’s safety equipment but are too embarrassed to ask!

Forgetful Flyers: Passengers have left some bizarre items in planes—including a live parrot, a glass eye, and even a potato with a smiley face drawn on it.

Long Layover Life: A man once lived in Paris Charles de Gaulle Airport for 18 years after being caught in immigration limbo. His story inspired the movie "The Terminal."

The Hotel of Books: In Japan, there’s a hotel where instead of walls, you’re surrounded by bookshelves. Perfect for bookworms who can’t get enough reading during vacation!
